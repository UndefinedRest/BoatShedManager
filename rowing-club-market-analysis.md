# Market Analysis: Boatshed Management Software for Australian Rowing Clubs

**Date:** October 30, 2025  
**Product:** Raspberry Pi-based boatshed management display solution integrating with RevolutioniseSport

## Executive Summary

This market analysis assesses the opportunity for a plug-and-play boatshed management display system targeting Australian rowing clubs using RevolutioniseSport. The solution addresses a clear pain point (poor at-a-glance fleet visibility) with a unique hardware-based approach.

**Key Findings:**
- **Total Addressable Market (TAM):** 156 rowing clubs across Australia
- **Serviceable Addressable Market (SAM):** 70-140 clubs (current + potential RevolutioniseSport users)
- **Estimated Year 1 Revenue:** $7,875-$23,625 (moderate scenario with 1.5 units per club)
- **Unit Economics:** $750 price point with $550-600 margin per unit (hardware cost ~$150-200)
- **Market Assessment:** Moderate to Good - Clear niche opportunity with limited scale potential

**Recommendation:** Proceed with cautious validation through pilot program before full productization.

## Pain Points & Product-Market Fit

### RevolutioniseSport Limitations for Rowing

While RevolutioniseSport excels at general sports club management, research reveals **critical gaps** for rowing-specific needs:

#### 1. Poor At-a-Glance Fleet Visualization

**The Problem:**
- RevolutioniseSport is web/mobile-first, not optimized for communal viewing
- Rowers need to see fleet availability quickly before heading to boathouse
- Current interface requires multiple clicks to view boat availability

**User Feedback from Rowing Community:**
> "It's perfect for allowing people to look from home at boat weights, who can use a particular boat, what boats are free, and any special instructions."
> 
> ‚Äî Townsville JCU Rowing Club user on Sports Notice Board

**Your Solution's Advantage:** Large TV display showing entire week's bookings at a glance

#### 2. Time Slot Complexity

**The Problem:**
- Rowing has very specific training windows: 5:00-7:30am and 4:00-6:30pm
- Weekend sessions at various times
- Generic booking systems don't optimize for these patterns

**Evidence:**
- Multiple clubs report needing "forward bookings up to 2 weeks in advance" with "specific time slots"
- Coaches need priority booking capabilities
- Equipment directors need to block boats for maintenance/regattas

#### 3. Boatshed-Specific Display Requirements

**The Problem:**
- Mobile-first systems assume users will check phones
- Boatshed reality: Users arrive, need immediate visual confirmation
- Weather-exposed environments require durable display solutions

**Your Solution's Advantage:** Physical, always-on display in the boatshed

#### 4. Self-Service Friction

**User Feedback:**
> "Because people can self-serve there are no complaints that the Boat Booking Officer hasn't gotten back to them to confirm a booking request."
> 
> ‚Äî Rowing club testimonial about effective booking systems

**Current Pain:** Email/message-based booking confirmation creates delays and frustration

**Current Pain:** Email-based booking creates volunteer burden and delays

**Implication:** Digital-only solutions miss the communal display requirement

#### 4. Coordination Failures

**User Quote:**
> "We inherited a system that is causing more confusion than needed with our masters rowers and the result is boats going unfilled or rowers missing a row while we have a club full of masters who would love more chances to hop into a team boat."

**Implication:** Poor visibility leads to underutilization of equipment
